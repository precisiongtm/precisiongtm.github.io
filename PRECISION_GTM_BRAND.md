# Precision GTM — Brand Guidelines

This file is the single source of truth for all Precision GTM content, design, and communication. Apply it to every asset: website, newsletter, LinkedIn posts, emails, decks, one-pagers, and AI-generated content.

---

## 1. Logo

**Wordmark:** `PRECISION GTM`

- All caps, no exceptions
- "PRECISION" in primary ink color
- "GTM" in cyan accent (`#00B8D9`)
- Font: DM Sans, weight 700, letter-spacing 0.14em
- No icon substitutes the wordmark in primary usage
- Minimum clear space: equal to the cap-height of the "P" on all sides
- Never stretch, rotate, recolor, or add effects to the wordmark

**Usage examples:**
```
PRECISION GTM   ← correct
Precision Gtm   ← wrong
precision gtm   ← wrong
PrecisionGTM    ← wrong
```

## 4. Writing Voice

### The Core Rule
**Operator voice, not consultant voice.** Every sentence should answer: "How do I know this person has done it?"

### Tense
- Past tense for things that were built or run: "built," "ran," "cut," "shipped," "closed"
- Present tense only for general statements of what Precision GTM does as a firm

### Approved Verbs
`built` `ran` `cut` `shipped` `closed` `rebuilt` `rewrote` `tested` `told` `fixed` `identified` `killed` `concentrated` `reduced` `designed` `developed` `delivered`

### Forbidden Verbs
`help` `define` `clarify` `align` `refine` `prioritize` `connect` `support` `enable` `explore` `assist` `leverage` `facilitate` `streamline` `utilize`

### Forbidden Words and Phrases
- Em dashes (never, under any circumstances -- use a comma, period, or rewrite)
- `inflection point`
- `compare notes`
- `delve` / `delve into`
- `tapestry`
- `leverage`
- `utilize` (always use "use")
- `testament`
- `landscape` (as in "today's digital landscape")
- `nuance` / `nuanced`
- `revolutionize` / `game-changer`
- `embark`
- `facilitate` / `streamline`
- `synergies`
- `thought leadership`
- `best-in-class`
- `robust`

### Sentence Style
- Short sentences. One idea per sentence.
- Break complex thoughts into pieces.
- Write like you are talking to a smart peer, not presenting to a board.
- Avoid contrasting structures like "It is not this, it is that."
- No padding. No filler. Every sentence earns its place.
- Use "we" on the website and in company content. Use "I" in personal posts and emails.

### Lead With the Decision, Not the Process
- Wrong: "We help companies define their ICP and align their narrative."
- Right: "We identified the wedge, cut the low-velocity segments, and rebuilt the outbound motion."

### Proof Over Description
- Wrong: "Experienced GTM leader with a track record of results."
- Right: "Built the partner motion from scratch. 30% of bookings ran through it within a year."

## 5. Content Structure Principles

### Case Studies
Always use this structure:
1. **Opening quote** -- the situation, in past tense, italicized
2. **What Changed** -- past-tense bullets, each a discrete action
3. **Commercial Impact** -- one or two sentences, directional outcome

### Section Labels
Short, all-caps, cyan. Always above the headline. Examples:
- `WHAT WE RAN`
- `CASE STUDIES`
- `GTM FRICTION AUDIT`
- `HOW AN ENGAGEMENT WORKS`

### Headlines
- Lead with the decision made or the outcome, not the service offered
- Serif font, -0.015em letter spacing
- Sentence case (not title case)

### Calls to Action
- Primary: direct and specific -- "Request a Diagnostic", "Request the Audit", "Watch Demo"
- Never: "Learn More", "Get Started", "Click Here"

## 7. Layout Principles (Web)

- **Max width:** 1080px centered
- **Page background:** Paper (`#F5F2EC`)
- **Section padding:** `clamp(4rem, 8vw, 7rem)` top/bottom
- **Border/divider color:** Rule (`#C9C0B0`)
- **Card grid:** 1px gap between cards, background set to Rule color (creates hairline border effect)
- **Dark sections** (e.g. How It Works): full-width Ink background, paper text, cyan labels
- **Border radius:** 2--3px maximum. Never large rounded corners.
- **No drop shadows** on cards or sections.
- **No gradients** except subtle radial glow on hero (cyan, 6% opacity max).
