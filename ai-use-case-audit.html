Most AI marketing projects do not fail in the build. They fail in the choosing.

Every marketing team has a list of AI use cases. Almost none has a defensible reason for what to do first. I help you pick, prove it to a CFO, and get to a working workflow without a six-month program.

The problem

The list is not the hard part.

Ask any marketing team for AI ideas and you will get twenty. Ask which one to build first and the room goes quiet, or worse, it goes to whoever argues loudest. Meanwhile the roadmap slips another quarter because cross-functional prioritization keeps stalling and nobody wants to be the one who picked wrong.

The projects that die do not die because the technology failed. They die because the data underneath was never clean, or the field never adopted the output, or nobody could build it once the enthusiasm faded, or no one could prove to finance that it worked. Those are the four things worth arguing about before you write a line of anything.

That is the entire job. Not the list. The choosing, and the proof.

Fit

Who this works for, and who it does not.

This is for you if
  • You lead marketing at a B2B software company, roughly $20M to $150M in revenue.
  • You have a list of AI ideas and no defensible way to choose between them.
  • Your team is 15 to 60 people with no dedicated AI function.
  • You need to show a result a CFO will credit, not activity metrics.
  • You can get your leadership team in a room for ninety minutes.
This is not for you if
  • You have an internal AI team already running a roadmap. You do not need me, and I would be a duplicate.
  • You want someone to build and run production systems. That is the partner's job, not mine.
  • Your CRM is unreliable and you are hoping AI will paper over it. Fix the data first; I will tell you that for free.
  • You want a strategy deck for the board. There are better and more expensive firms for that.
  • Nobody in your organization can build or maintain anything. Start with hiring, not with me.
